Projects

Below are selected supervised and independent research projects focused on proactive online safety, toxicity forecasting, and cyberbullying detection.

SpotTox — AI-Powered Toxicity Detection Web Application

In Fall 2025, I supervised a senior capstone team that developed SpotTox, an interactive web platform for early detection and analysis of toxic conversations.

  • Supports multiple inputs: text, threads, URLs, and screenshots.
  • Integrates Transformer-based models (BERT, RoBERTa, DistilBERT, LSTM).
  • Provides dashboards for toxicity trends and flagged comments.
  • Designed as an early-warning tool beyond single-post moderation systems.

Independent Undergraduate Research Supervision

In Spring 2025, I supervised Krisha Patel as an independent undergraduate researcher under my broader project on forecasting toxicity escalation in social media threads.

  • Explored early-stage conversational cues for cyberbullying detection.
  • Studied escalation and withdrawal patterns in Reddit discussions.
  • Contributed toward proactive moderation and intervention frameworks.

Ongoing Research Direction

My current PhD research continues to expand on early toxicity forecasting by combining temporal modeling, interpretable machine learning, and trajectory-based conversation analysis to support safer online communities.
